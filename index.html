Overview

Quick guide to give you an idea of the battle ahead.

The most important things you need to know when applying for VA benefits: YOU DESERVE THIS, and YOU ARE NOT TAKING MONEY FROM OTHER VETS BY RECEIVING BENEFITS. The money the VA gives to disabled veterans comes from a pool that contains other, much less important federal programs. We will never get to the bottom of the VA benefits pool, in fact every year there's so much money left over it gets absorbed by those other programs. Were you near loud stuff? 10% rating for the rest of your life. Were you around burn pits? 0% forever, which means free allergy medicine for you and your family.

If you're still active duty, get the ball rolling on attaining a physical copy of your service medical record. You will also have the option of requsting your medical record on CD through eVetRecs after you seperate, but be warned they will remove information before mailing you that disk.

Once you start the process of getting your files from your respective branch, you will now need to create an eBenefits and myHealthEVet accounts. If you still have access to your CAC, be sure to create a DS LOGON account as well. myHealthEVet now allows login.gov sign-in, so if you're already using USAJOBS.GOV (if not, you should be) then you can use that login.

If you're seperated, you need to get registered with your local VA and start the process of getting approved for a Premium account on myHealthEVet. This will involve driving to your nearest VA and filling out a 1010-EZ form initially, then setting up a first visit. The process for getting the Premium certification on your myHealthEVet account can vary wildly depending on your VA - be prepared for a headache. Also this will eventually get you the sacred VHID, which will allow NEX/PX access.

Once you have access to eBenefits, myHealthEVet, and are registered in the VA system, your next step is to get set up with either the DAV or if you're in Texas, the TVC. This process will involve touching base with a representative, filling out some paperwork, and giving them PoA on the eBenefits portal. Fair warning: I have heard some real nightmares from the DAV, and because they are a national outfit you can get all kinds when dealing with them. Pro tip here is to just ask for a different case manager if you're not getting anywhere - they're a non-profit existing entirely to help veterans, if they're not helping you need to let them know.

At this point, you may be wondering why we should have to go through all of this trouble when the VA already has a system in place to "easliy" apply for benefits with just one visit to your local care facility. The answer is: they will fuck you. While the individuals at the VA may be helpful, caring vets themselves, the machine is a lot more sinister. By side-stepping the VA benefits application and appeals process, you are saving yourself at least 18 months of work and guaranteeing a higher percentage. This is accomplished by the veteran representative agency having your diagnosis done by a non-VA doctor and in a timely manner.

USAJOBS

Here's some info to get you started on getting that USAJOB interview, or 'How to Get Past the Bots and Love the SF-86'.

FASCLASS

Use this website to search for your respective military job expereience at the level you're qualified for by filling in your military job title in the 'Position Title' bar and clicking 'Search', then use the job descriptions contained on their website to build your bullet points for your work experience. Junior enlisted should stay below GS-09, senior enlisted shoot for GS-10 to GS-12. Officers with STEM/Medcial degrees can take from the GS-12 and GS-13 positions.

Resume Template

This is my personal template I built while attending a paid career bootcamp. It has since landed me three federal jobs, one as a direct hire and two as a contractor. I have removed some personal information for obvious reasons, but the skeleton is there for you to use at your discretion.

Grammerly

Grammerly is a resource to improve writing quality and can be use to change the verbiage and sentence structure of the FASCLASS job descriptions that you pulled for your resume. The hiring managers that will inevitably see your application on USAJOBS will immediately toss your resume in the trash if they see that you just copy/pasted from any common resource. How we get past this is to feed that information into Grammerly, while keeping all of the keywords and tricky phrases generated by FASCLASS. If you're proficient enough with the English language, or don't mind digging through a thesaurus, you can attempt to put you work history into your own words.
